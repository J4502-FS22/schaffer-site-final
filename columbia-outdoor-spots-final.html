7 Outdoor Recreation Spots Around Columbia

At first, Columbia, located smack dab in the middle of Missouri, might seem like a pretty unassuming college town. When I first moved out here, I felt trapped by its relatively small size. Then, I discovered just how awesome its central location is in terms of outdoor recreation. Here are some of my favorite spots that have helped me come to love the area.

Big Muddy National Wildlife Refuge

Brady Lane
Booneville, MO
65233
(660) 672-2806

Visit Big Muddy National Wildlife Refuge's website

As one of 588 National Wildlife Refuges in the country, Big Muddy is a space for the public to enjoy wildlife viewing, hiking, fishing, hunting, boating, and even geocaching. This area along the Missouri River is carefully managed to make sure the ecosystem's health comes first.

Kiwanis Park

926 College Park Dr
Columbia, MO
65203
(573) 874-7460

Visit Kiwanis Park's website

This gem is hidden right in the suburbs of Columbia and is a great hiking place for beginners. There are many trails and connector options so you can walk a distance tailored to your time. It is also the center of a large honeysuckle eradication operation to get rid of the invasive plant.

Providence North Area Rock Climbing

9741 Old Plank Rd S
Columbia, MO
65203
(573) 554-5894

Visit "The Mountain Project" climbing guide website

Get ready to get your blood pumping! This outdoor climbing spot is great for people just getting started with outdoor climbing and experienced sport climbers alike. If you are more used to top rope climbing, make sure to bring a buddy who can set the route for you on lead.

Rock Bridge Memorial State Park

5901 South Highway 163
Columbia, MO
65203
(573) 449-7402

Visit Missouri State Parks's website

If you are unfamiliar with Missouri's unique geology and topography, look no further than Rock Bridge. Signs along the park's main trail describe how the landscape lined with caves, cliffs, ravines, and sinkholes were created with natural, chemical reactions in the limestone.

Finger Lakes State Park

1505 E. Peabody Road
Columbia, MO
65202
(573) 443-5315

Visit Missouri State Parks's website

This is a popular camping area for locals just looking to spend a night out under the stars. Reservable campsites are accessible by vehicle and have pre-made fire rings. Finger Lakes is also great for a quick day hike through hilly woods or a hop in your canoe.

Pinnacles Youth Park

850 E Pinnacles Rd
Sturgeon, MO
65284
(573) 449-7946

Visit Pinnacles Youth Park's website

Perhaps one of the more famous outdoor spots in the state, the Pinnacles have trails leading up to towering pillars of rock that were once part of an underwater canyon. Try to hit the park earlier in the morning or on a weekday to avoid large groups of families.

Capen Park

1600 Capen Park Dr
Columbia, MO
65201
(573) 874-7460

Visit Capen Park's website

For students at the University of Missouri, this is the closest outdoor climbing spot. Anchors bolted into the top of the cliff make it easy for beginners to hike up, tie in, repel down, and climb the routes on top rope.
